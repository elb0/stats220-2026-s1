1. Embrace the Error Message

In STATS 220, you'll see a lot of red text. Don't panic! An error message isn't a failure; it's a conversation. R is just telling you it didn't understand the syntax. Copy-paste that error into Google or Ed Discussion—chances are, someone else has been there too.

2. Trust the Tidyverse

The tidyverse is your best friend. Whether you're using ggplot2 for visualizations or dplyr for data manipulation, remember the power of the pipe (%>% or |>). It keeps your code logical and readable. Keep your data "long" and your spirit high!

3. CSS & HTML are Creative Tools

Don't just view web tech as "more coding." View it as a way to showcase your data stories. A well-formatted div or a clean CSS layout makes your hard-earned insights much more impactful for your audience.

4. Use Your Resources

Between Canvas, Ed Discussion, and the labs, you have a massive support network. STATS 220 is about problem-solving, not just memorizing functions. Ask questions early and often.
